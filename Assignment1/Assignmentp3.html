The V-Shaped Model

The V-Shaped model is an extension of the waterfall model, instead of moving down in a linear way,
the process steps are bent upwards after the coding phase, to form the typical V shape. The major difference between V-Shaped model and Waterfall model is the early test planning in the V-Shaped model.



Advantages Disadvantages
  • Higher chance of success over the waterfall model due to the development of
    test plans early on during the life cylce.
  • Works well when requirements are easily understood.
  • Verification and valifation of the product is found in early stages of product
    development.
  • No early prototypes of the software are produced because
    software is developed during the implementation phase.
  • The model doesn't provide a clear path for problems found during testing
    phases.
  • It costs more and requires more time, in addition to detailed plan.
