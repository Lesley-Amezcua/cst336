The Spiral Model

The piral model combines the idea of iterative development with the systematic, controlled aspects of the waterfall model.
Spiral model is a combination of iterative development process model and sequential linear development model i.e. waterfall model with very high emphasis on risk analysis. the spiral model is favored for large, expensive, and complicated projects. This model uses the same phases as the waterfall model, in pretty much the same order, separated by planning, risk assessment, and the building of protoypes and simulations. The Spiral model has four phases. They include identification, design, construct or build, and evaluation and risk analysis.



Advantages Disadvantages
  • Estimates like budget and schedule become more realistic as work progresses
    because important issues are discovered earlier.
  • Manages risks and develops the system into phases.
  • Developers get involved earlier than other models.
  • Needs special skills to evaluate the risks and assumptions.
  • It is costly and required more time to reach the final product.
  • It is highly customized that it limits reusability.
